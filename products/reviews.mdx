# Reviews

Customers rate products 1–5 stars and leave comments. Reviews go through a **moderation workflow** before appearing publicly, and admins can reply to each one.

> The dashboard list view of all reviews is in development. For now, reviews are managed per-product (from the product detail page) or via the API.

---

## What a Review Holds

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Rating** | 1–5 stars (required) |
| **Title** | Short headline (e.g. "Comfortable, runs small") |
| **Comment** | Body of the review |
| **Author** | The customer who wrote it (linked to Customers module) |
| **Verified purchase** | True if the customer's order history includes this product |
| **Helpful count** | Other customers' "this was helpful" votes |
| **Status** | `pending`, `approved`, or `rejected` |
| **Reply** | Optional admin response (content + repliedAt timestamp) |

---

## Lifecycle

```
Customer writes review  →  status: pending
                              ↓
Moderator reviews          ┌──────────────┐
                           ↓              ↓
                       approved       rejected
                           ↓              ↓
                   Shows on storefront   Hidden, customer informed (optional)
                           ↓
            Counts toward average rating
```

By default, reviews start in `pending`. Some businesses configure auto-approve (skip moderation entirely) — most retailers leave it on for spam protection.

---

## Approve a Review

1. Open the product → **Reviews** tab
2. Find the pending review
3. Click **Approve**

The review is now visible on the storefront. Its rating counts toward the product's `stats.averageRating` (recalculated on every approve/delete).

---

## Reject a Review

For spam, abusive language, or off-topic content:

1. Open the product → **Reviews** tab
2. Click **Reject** on the bad review
3. Optionally add a private note for audit ("Spam — repeated phrase")

Rejected reviews:
- Don't appear on the storefront
- Don't count toward average rating
- Stay in the database for audit + future re-review

You can re-approve a rejected review later if needed.

---

## Reply to a Review

Admins (and customer-service roles) can reply publicly to any **approved** review:

1. Open the review → **Reply** button
2. Type your reply (markdown supported)
3. Save

The reply appears under the review on the storefront with a "Store reply" badge. Use replies to:

- Thank customers for positive feedback
- Address concerns in negative reviews ("Sorry to hear about the size — we're updating our chart")
- Provide product-use tips ("Try washing on cold to preserve color")

Replying to a review **auto-approves it** if it was still pending.

---

## Verified Purchases

The `verifiedPurchase` flag is set automatically when:

- The author's customer record has a completed order containing this product
- The order is `delivered` or `paid` status (not pending/cancelled)

Verified reviews carry more weight on the storefront — they typically display a **green checkmark** badge. Customers trust them more.

For pre-launch reviews (e.g. press, influencers reviewing before launch), you can manually set `verifiedPurchase: true` via the API — but most stores keep this strict to avoid trust erosion.

## Average Rating + Distribution

The product's `stats.averageRating` is the mean of all approved reviews (1.0–5.0).

Some storefront UIs also show the **distribution**:

```
5 stars: ████████░░ 80% (40 reviews)
4 stars: ██░░░░░░░░ 15% (8)
3 stars: ░░░░░░░░░░  3% (1)
2 stars: ░░░░░░░░░░  2% (1)
1 star:  ░░░░░░░░░░  0% (0)
```

This helps customers understand if the average is consistent (mostly 5★) or polarized (lots of 5★ AND 1★).

---

## Helpful Votes

Customers can mark reviews as "helpful" via the storefront. The `helpfulCount` increments each time. Sort the review list by helpfulness to surface the most useful reviews first — typically more useful than chronological order.

The system tracks who voted to prevent duplicate votes from the same customer.

---

## Common Patterns

**"Auto-approve known good customers"** — possible with custom logic: check if author's customer has > N approved past reviews → auto-approve. Configure with your platform admin.

**"Email customers when their review is approved"** — wire up via the events bus (`product:review.approved` event → notification). Useful for engagement, optional.

**"Negative reviews need management response"** — internal SLA: any review ≤ 2★ requires a reply within 48 hours. The reply doesn't need to fix the issue, just acknowledge and offer next steps.

---

## Reporting

Common queries (via API or dashboard):

- **Pending review backlog** — `?status=pending` count → moderation queue size
- **Average rating per product** — sort products by `stats.averageRating` desc
- **Low-rated products** — `?stats.averageRating<3` to find candidates for review or delisting
- **Verified vs unverified ratio** — measure trust signal strength
- **Reply rate** — % of approved reviews that have a reply (proxy for customer-service engagement)

---

## Common Pitfalls

| Symptom | Cause + fix |
|---------|-------------|
| Average rating doesn't match what I'm seeing | Average uses only approved reviews. Pending/rejected don't count. |
| Customer wrote a review but I don't see it | Filter the Reviews tab by status — it's likely `pending`. |
| Verified-purchase flag missing on a real customer | Their customer record might not be linked to the order. Check the order's customerId. |
| Spam reviews accumulating | Disable auto-approve, train moderators, consider rate limiting per IP/customer |
