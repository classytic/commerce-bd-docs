# SME / TOT basics

Your annual turnover is between **BDT 50 lakh and BDT 3 crore**, so NBR
classifies you as an SME operating under the **Turnover Tax (TOT)** regime.

## What TOT means

- You pay a **flat 4%** of your taxable turnover every month.
- That's it. No charging customers 15% VAT, no reclaiming input VAT,
  no per-line rate calculations.
- You file **Mushak 9.2** (not 9.1), due the 15th of the following month.

## What the system does automatically

1. **POS / sales**: system posts revenue + TOT 4% to `2132.TOT4.COLLECTED`.
   You never see VAT lines on receipts — just the gross sale.
2. **Purchases**: whatever VAT your supplier charged is **folded into
   inventory cost** — because you can't claim it back. Your cost goes up
   by that amount. This is correct NBR behavior for TOT regime.
3. **Monthly return**: open `/dashboard/accounting/tot-return`, pick the
   month, click Export CSV — hand it to your NBR filing agent.

## What's different from standard VAT

| | Standard VAT | TOT (SME) |
|---|---|---|
| Charge customer | 15% on top | Nothing extra |
| Claim input VAT | Yes | No |
| Filing form | Mushak 9.1 | Mushak 9.2 |
| Exempt items | Shown separately | Excluded from TOT base |

## Common questions

**Can I issue Mushak 6.3 invoices?**
No — TOT registrants don't issue 6.3. Your POS receipts and regular
invoices are sufficient.

**What if I sell exempt items (rice, vegetables)?**
Mark them as EXEMPT_STAPLE / EXEMPT_VEGETABLE in the product tax class.
The system excludes exempt turnover from the 4% TOT base automatically.

**I crossed BDT 3 crore — what now?**
You have to migrate to STANDARD_VAT this month. Go to Platform Settings →
Branches → Edit branch → change Business Type to STANDARD_VAT. The next
monthly return will be 9.1. Speak to a CA first — there's usually some
transition reconciliation.
