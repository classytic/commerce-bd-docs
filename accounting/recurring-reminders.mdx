# Recurring Invoices & Payment Reminders

Set up subscription billing and automatic payment follow-ups.

---

## Recurring invoices

For monthly subscriptions, rent, or any repeating charge:

1. Create a **template invoice** with the line items and amount
2. Go to **Invoices** > **Recurring** and click **Create Recurring Config**
3. Choose:
   - **Frequency** — daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, yearly
   - **Start date** — when to begin
   - **End date** — when to stop (optional, leave blank for indefinite)
   - **Auto-post** — post the invoice immediately (or leave as draft for review)
   - **Auto-send** — email the invoice to the customer on creation

The system generates a new invoice on schedule. Each generated invoice is linked to the recurring config so you can track the history.

> **Stripe/payment provider?** If you use Stripe or another billing system, you can link the recurring config to the external subscription ID. The system tracks both.

---

## Payment reminders (dunning)

When invoices go overdue, the system can send reminders automatically.

### Default schedule

| Days relative to due date | What happens |
|---------------------------|--------------|
| **-3 days** (before due) | Friendly reminder: "Your invoice is due in 3 days" |
| **Due date** | "Your invoice is due today" |
| **+7 days** | First overdue notice |
| **+14 days** | Second overdue notice |
| **+30 days** | Final notice |

Each reminder is tracked — the invoice shows how many reminders were sent and when.

### Grace period

By default, there is a **3-day grace period** after the due date before the system marks an invoice as overdue. This prevents false alarms for invoices that are paid a day or two late.

---

## Late fees

If enabled, the system can automatically create a separate late fee invoice for overdue amounts:

- **Rate** — percentage of overdue amount (default: 2%)
- **Period** — charge once, daily, or monthly
- **Grace days** — days after due date before late fees start

Late fee invoices are linked to the original invoice. They appear as separate line items in your reports.

> Late fees are disabled by default. Ask your admin to enable them if needed.

---

## Manual triggers

You can trigger dunning or recurring processing manually:

1. Go to **Invoices**
2. Click **Process Dunning** to send reminders for all overdue invoices now
3. Click **Process Recurring** to generate all scheduled invoices now

This is useful for testing or when you need to catch up after a system pause.
