# RMG exporter basics

You operate an export-oriented garments factory with a bonded-warehouse
licence. NBR gives you special treatment: zero-rated exports, deferred
VAT on imported raw material, and utility VAT exemption.

## The three things that make you different

### 1. Raw material imports → bonded warehouse

When fabric/yarn arrives at customs, VAT is **deferred** (not exempt —
just postponed). It stays deferred as long as the material is consumed
to produce exported garments. This is how your Utilization Declaration
(UD) works.

- Customs posts the import to `1154 Deferred Input VAT` (when we
  implement bonded-warehouse posting — currently the stack uses `1150.VAT15.INPUT`).
- On export, no VAT event.
- On DTA (domestic) sale of the same material — VAT triggers retrospectively.

### 2. Exports are zero-rated

- Customer country ≠ BD → system auto-remaps `STANDARD` → `ZERO_EXPORT`
  via the INTERNATIONAL fiscal position.
- The invoice still posts — just with 0% VAT.
- Input VAT you paid on utilities, office supplies, etc. is **refundable**
  through the 1150.VAT0.INPUT account (see `/dashboard/accounting/export-refund`).

### 3. Utility VAT exemption (SRO-190/2023)

Gas, electricity, and telecom suppliers who serve your factory are
exempt from charging VAT on those utilities. When the supplier issues a
bill flagged as "utility → RMG factory", the system applies the
RMG_UTILITY fiscal position — no VAT line.

**You still need to keep copies of those bills.** The SRO requires proof.

## What the system does automatically

- **Foreign-buyer invoice**: fiscal position = INTERNATIONAL, VAT = 0%,
  line on 1150.VAT0.INPUT accumulates a refund claim.
- **Domestic sale (DTA)**: full 15% VAT charged. Separate tracking so
  bonded-warehouse UD reconciliation works.
- **Utility bill**: detect via `suppliesUtility: true` + `buyer.isRmgFactory`
  → RMG_UTILITY fiscal position, no VAT charged.
- **Monthly return**: Mushak 9.1 with zero-rated sales called out on line 7.

## Audit trail

Every exemption leaves a record on the Mushak invoice:
- `fiscalPosition`: which remap applied
- `sroReference`: SRO-190/2023 (utility), export manifest (foreign sale)
- `exemptionReason`: plain-text human log

NBR audits RMG exporters heavily — this trail is load-bearing.

## Common questions

**Can I buy domestic raw material?**
Yes — but the VAT on that purchase is claimable as regular input VAT
(1150.VAT15.INPUT), not deferred. You claim it back through your
monthly return.

**What if export shipment is cancelled and sold domestically?**
DTA sale — posts at 15% VAT, the deferred/export-refund adjustments
need manual entry. Talk to your CA.
