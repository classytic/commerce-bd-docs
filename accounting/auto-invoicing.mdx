# Auto-Invoicing

The system can create invoices automatically when sales, purchases, or POS transactions happen. You control the policy per branch.

---

## Sales invoices (from orders)

When a customer places an order, the system can auto-create an invoice.

| Policy | What happens |
|--------|--------------|
| **Off** (default) | No auto-invoice. Create manually from the invoices page. |
| **On Order** | Invoice created as draft when order is confirmed. Post manually. |
| **On Payment** | Invoice created and posted when customer pays. Fully automatic. |

> Set the policy in your branch settings or ask your admin to set the `INVOICE_AUTO_SALES` env variable.

---

## Vendor bills (from purchases)

When you receive goods from a supplier, the system can auto-create a bill.

| Policy | What happens |
|--------|--------------|
| **Off** (default) | No auto-bill. Create manually. |
| **On Receive** | Bill created as draft when goods are received in warehouse. |

---

## POS receipts

For point-of-sale transactions, receipts can be generated automatically.

| Policy | What happens |
|--------|--------------|
| **Off** (default) | No auto-receipt. |
| **Receipt per Transaction** | One receipt per POS sale. Immediate, no batching. |
| **Receipt per Day** | One consolidated receipt at day-close. Less paperwork. |

> POS receipts post immediately — they don't need manual posting since cash is collected at the time of sale.

---

## How it works technically

Auto-invoicing listens to events from other modules:

- **Order confirmed** event -> creates customer invoice (if policy is `on_order`)
- **Payment received** event -> creates and posts customer invoice (if policy is `on_payment`)
- **Goods received** event -> creates vendor bill (if policy is `on_receive`)
- **POS day-close** event -> creates consolidated receipt (if policy is `receipt_per_day`)

All auto-created invoices are linked back to their source document (order ID, purchase ID, etc.) so you can always trace where they came from.
