# VAT Basics — How It Works Here

A quick, plain-language tour of how VAT flows through the system. Written for shop staff, not accountants.

---

## The short version

Every sale and purchase touches VAT automatically. You don't calculate anything by hand — the system handles it. Your job is to know where it appears so you can check it's right and fix it when it's wrong.

---

## The four things that happen

### 1. You sell something at the POS

When a cashier rings up a sale:

- Customer pays the full price (VAT included).
- The system **splits** the amount into revenue + VAT internally.
- VAT goes to account **2132 — VAT Output Payable** (money you owe NBR).
- Revenue goes to account **4111 — Domestic Sales**.

You don't do anything extra. It's automatic.

### 2. You receive a purchase from a supplier

When stock arrives and you mark the purchase as **received**:

- Inventory goes up by the **net** amount (price before VAT).
- The **input VAT** portion goes to account **1150 — VAT Input Receivable** (NBR owes this back to you as a credit).
- Accounts Payable (2111) goes up by the **full** amount (what you owe the supplier).

> **Tip:** If the supplier didn't give you a Mushak 6.3 invoice, you cannot claim input VAT. Make sure purchase entries have a `taxRate` set only when you have the paper.

### 3. You transfer stock between your own branches

Same company, different branches (same BIN) is **not a sale** to NBR. The system creates a **Mushak 6.5 transfer challan**, not a VAT invoice.

- No VAT event. No output VAT. No input VAT.
- Stock just moves from branch A to branch B.
- Cost layers follow the stock.

Different legal entities (different BIN) **is** a sale. Those use Mushak 6.3 with VAT.

### 4. Month ends — you file Mushak 9.1

Go to **Accounting → VAT Return (9.1)** on the 1st–15th of the next month.

- The system adds up all your output VAT (from sales).
- Subtracts your input VAT credit (from purchases).
- Shows **Net Payable**.
- You pay that amount to NBR by the 15th.

## VAT rates you'll see

| Rate | Use it for | Input credit? |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **15% (STANDARD)** | Most goods and services | Yes |
| **7.5% (REDUCED_7_5)** | Non-AC restaurant, RMG retail | No |
| **10%, 5%** | Specific sectors per SRO | No |
| **0% (ZERO)** | Exports, EPZ supplies | Yes (refundable) |
| **EXEMPT** | Fresh veg, medicine, education | No |

> **Remember:** Only 15% and 0% rates let you claim input credit. If your supplier invoices you at 7.5%, that VAT becomes part of your inventory cost — you can't get it back.

## The Mushak 6.3 invoice (VAT invoice)

Every sale over BDT 1 lakh to a VAT-registered buyer **must** have a Mushak 6.3. It's the tax invoice.

### Who issues it?
- **POS orders** — you click Generate Mushak on the order detail page.
- **Online orders** — same.
- **Manual** — for invoices outside the order system.

### Serial format
`BRANCHCODE/YYYY/000001` — sequential per branch per year, no gaps allowed by NBR.

### If it's wrong
Don't delete. **Cancel it** (on the list page). The serial stays reserved; a cancelled invoice doesn't count toward your output VAT. Issue a new one if needed.

## Common "why is this happening?" questions

### "Why did my net payable jump this month?"
You either sold more (output VAT up) or received fewer purchase invoices (input credit down). Check the **Input VAT Credit** number on the VAT Return page — if it's lower than usual, go find missing purchases.

### "My supplier gave me VAT but the system shows 0 input credit."
Three common causes:
1. The purchase was entered with `taxRate: 0` by mistake. Fix on the purchase page before receiving.
2. The rate is a truncated one (7.5%, 5%, 10%) — these don't allow input claim by law.
3. The purchase wasn't marked "received" yet. The accounting entry fires on **receive**, not on create.

### "I sold something but it's not on the VAT return."
- Check the Mushak 6.3 was generated (go to Musok list, search by order number).
- Check its status isn't **cancelled** — cancelled invoices are excluded.
- Check the date matches the return period (sale on April 1 goes on April return, not March).

### "A cashier voided a sale. What happens?"
A refund journal entry is posted automatically — output VAT goes **down** in 2132 for that amount. The original Mushak 6.3 can also be cancelled (recommended for audit trail).

## What staff can override

You can, with the right role:

- **Cancel** a Mushak 6.3 — `finance_admin` or `admin`.
- **Edit purchase taxRate** before marking it received — `inventory_staff` or higher.
- **Regenerate** a Mushak 6.3 for the same order — idempotent, returns the original if already exists.

You cannot:

- Post directly to VAT accounts (2132, 1150). All VAT entries must come from sales/purchases/refunds.
- Skip a Mushak serial number. If you cancel invoice 42, the next one is 43 (42 stays cancelled).
- Change rates after a sale is posted. Fix by cancelling the Mushak and issuing a new one.
