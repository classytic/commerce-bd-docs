# Withholding Certificates (VDS / TDS)

## What are withholding certificates?

When a business pays a supplier or receives payment from a buyer, tax may be deducted at the source of payment. The party that withholds the tax issues a certificate to prove the deduction was made.

- **VDS (VAT Deducted at Source):** A buyer withholds VAT from a supplier's invoice before paying. Common for services rendered to corporate clients or government bodies.
- **TDS (Tax Deducted at Source):** A buyer withholds income tax from a supplier's payment. The rate depends on the TDS section applicable to the transaction.

## When you ISSUE a certificate

You issue a certificate when **you withhold tax from a supplier payment**. For example, you hire a consulting firm and pay them BDT 100,000. If the VDS rate is 10%, you withhold BDT 10,000 and pay the supplier BDT 90,000. You then issue them a withholding certificate for BDT 10,000.

## When you RECEIVE a certificate

You receive a certificate when **a buyer withholds tax from your payment**. For example, a corporate client purchases goods from you worth BDT 200,000 and withholds BDT 10,000 as VDS. They pay you BDT 190,000 and hand you a certificate. You can claim credit for this withheld amount when filing your VAT or income tax return.

## How to record a certificate

1. Go to **Accounting > Withholding Certs** in the sidebar.
2. Click **New Certificate**.
3. Select the **Type** (VDS or TDS) and **Direction** (Issued or Received).
4. Enter the **Certificate Number**, **Date**, and **Period** (month in YYYY-MM format).
5. Fill in the **Counterparty Name** and **TIN** (Taxpayer Identification Number).
6. Enter the **Gross Amount** (total before deduction), **Rate**, **Withholding Amount**, and **Net Paid**.
7. Optionally add a **Challan Number** if the deposit challan is available, and any **Notes**.
8. Click **Create Certificate**.

## How to reconcile certificates

Before filing your Mushak 9.1 VAT return or income tax return, you should reconcile all certificates for the period:

1. Filter the list by the relevant **Period** and **Type**.
2. Review each unreconciled certificate against supporting documents (invoices, challans, bank statements).
3. Click the **Reconcile** button on each verified certificate.
4. Once reconciled, the certificate is marked green and will not appear in the unreconciled count.

## Checking unreconciled totals

The summary cards at the top of the page show:

- **Total Issued** -- aggregate amount of certificates you have issued.
- **Total Received** -- aggregate amount of certificates you have received.
- **Unreconciled** -- count of certificates not yet reconciled. This number should be zero before you file your return.
