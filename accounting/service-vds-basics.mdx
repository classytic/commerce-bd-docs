# Service provider & VDS basics

You provide services (consulting, design, advertising, security, cleaning,
construction, etc.) and bill corporate clients. NBR requires those clients
to **withhold VAT at source** when paying you — this is VDS (VAT Deducted
at Source).

## What VDS means for you

1. You invoice the client: **service value + 15% VAT** (as normal).
2. At payment time, the client deducts VDS from what they pay you.
3. Client deposits the VDS directly to NBR.
4. Client issues you a **Mushak 6.6 certificate** as proof.
5. You claim the VDS as a credit against your own Mushak 9.1 line 15.

**Net cash flow**: you receive the service value; the client pays NBR
the VAT. The VDS certificate gets you the credit on filing.

## Example — BDT 100,000 professional consulting

```
  Invoice:           BDT 100,000
  + VAT 15%:         BDT  15,000
  = Gross:           BDT 115,000

Client (CORP) deducts VDS @ 15% of service value:
  VDS withheld:      BDT  15,000

  Client pays you:   BDT 100,000   (gross − VDS)
  Client pays NBR:   BDT  15,000
  You get 6.6 for:   BDT  15,000
```

Your own VAT liability that month: BDT 15,000 output − 15,000 VDS credit
= 0 due. (Simplified — actual return also nets input VAT.)

## VDS rates (who deducts how much)

Depends on BOTH the **payer category** and the **service category**.
NBR publishes this matrix via SRO-240-Law/2021/163.

| Service | GOVT | CORP | BANK | NGO |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Professional consulting | 15% | 15% | 15% | 15% |
| Construction contractor | 7.5% | 7.5% | — | — |
| Security | 10% | 10% | — | — |
| Cleaning | 10% | 10% | — | — |
| Rent (commercial) | 15% | 15% | — | — |
| Catering | 10% | 10% | — | — |
| Printing | 10% | 10% | — | — |
| Advertising | 15% | 15% | — | — |
| IT / software | 5% | 5% | — | — |
| Transport / freight | 5% | 5% | — | — |

Our system looks up the correct rate via `bd-vat.getVdsRate(payer, service)`.

## What the system does automatically

- **You as seller**: invoice goes out at full 15%. When the client pays
  and sends Mushak 6.6, record it in `/dashboard/accounting/vds-receivable`
  — the 1153 VDS Receivable balance feeds line 15 of your 9.1 return.
- **You as buyer** (paying a service supplier): if your branch is
  corporate / govt / bank / NGO, you withhold VDS at payment time.
  The supplier-payment contract splits cash: part to supplier, part to
  2136 VDS Payable (to deposit to NBR within 15 days).

## Common questions

**What if the client doesn't send Mushak 6.6?**
Chase them. Your 1153 Receivable ages until you have the certificate.
Without it, you can't claim the credit on your 9.1 return — NBR will
reject the line.

**Do I charge VAT to clients under BDT 10 crore turnover?**
Yes — they still pay your 15% VAT. Only entities with turnover ≥
BDT 10 crore are VDS-withholding payers. Smaller clients pay you
the full gross.

**Deposit deadline?**
VDS withheld must be deposited to NBR within **15 days** of deduction.
Late deposits trigger penalties.
