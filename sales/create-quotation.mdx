# Create a Quotation

How to build a draft quotation from scratch.

---

## 1. Open the Quotations page

Go to **Orders > Quotations** in the sidebar. You'll see all quotes for the current branch.

[Open Quotations](/dashboard/quotations)

The list is filtered to your active branch (the one you have selected in the top bar). Switching branches changes which quotes you see — head office and each store keep their own quote book.

---

## 2. Click "New Quotation"

You'll be asked for:

### Customer

- **Existing customer** — search by name, phone, or email. The quote will link to their record.
- **Walk-in** — type the name and contact details directly. The quote saves a snapshot, so it works even before you create a full customer record.

The customer details on the quote are a **snapshot**. If you later edit the customer's profile, the quote keeps the original info. This is important for legal traceability — the quote shows what was agreed at the time.

### Channel

How the quote will reach the customer:

- **B2B** — formal business quote (default)
- **Web** — generated from a web inquiry
- **Phone** — phone-based negotiation
- **Walk-in** — counter or showroom

Channel is informational — it doesn't change behavior. Use it for reporting later.

---

## 3. Add line items

For each product:

1. Search by name or SKU
2. Set the quantity
3. Either accept the catalog price or override it

### Price overrides

If you're offering a quantity discount or bulk pricing, click on the unit price and type the agreed amount. The line total updates automatically.

The override is **per-line** and only applies to this quote. It does not change the catalog price.

### Sections and notes

For long quotes, you can add:

- **Section headers** — non-priced rows that group lines (e.g., "Phase 1", "Phase 2")
- **Note rows** — non-priced text, useful for warranty terms or delivery details

---

## 4. Set the validity date

Pick a date by which the customer needs to accept. Common values:

- **7 days** — short, urgent quotes
- **30 days** — standard B2B
- **90 days** — long lead-time products

After this date, the quote auto-expires (a background job changes its status). Expired quotes can't be accepted — you'd have to clone the quote into a new draft.

If you don't set a validity date, the quote never expires automatically. You can still expire it manually later.

---

## 5. Notes

Two kinds of notes:

- **Customer-facing notes** — appear on the quote PDF and the customer-facing view (warranty, payment terms, delivery promises)
- **Internal notes** — staff-only, never sent to the customer (negotiation history, follow-up reminders)

Both are searchable later from the quotations list.

---

## 6. Save as draft

Click **Create**. The quote is saved as a **Draft** with a number like `QUO-2026-0042`.

It's not sent to the customer yet — it's just sitting in your drafts. You can come back, edit it, or send it whenever you're ready.

[Go to Quotations](/dashboard/quotations)

---

## Quick tips

- **Drafts are editable.** Once a quote is sent, lines and prices are locked. Make sure the numbers are right before sending.
- **Customer email is required to send.** Drafts without an email can be saved but can't be sent until you fill in a contact.
- **Stock is not reserved.** Building a quote doesn't take stock out of inventory. Stock is only touched when the quote is accepted and converted to an order.
