# Quotations Overview

A **quotation** is a price offer you send to a customer before they commit. Think of it as a saved cart that the customer hasn't paid for yet — with a price, a validity window, and a paper trail.

Use quotations for:

- **B2B sales** where the buyer needs a written quote before approving the purchase
- **Custom orders** where the price depends on quantity discounts, freight, or specific terms
- **Tenders / RFQs** where you submit a formal price for a future order

If a customer is buying something off-the-shelf at the listed price, skip quotations and create an order directly. Quotations are for the cases where there's a negotiation step.

## How a quotation moves through its life

```
Draft ──▶ Sent ──▶ Viewed ──▶ Accepted ──▶ Converted (becomes an order)
                              │
                              ├──▶ Rejected (customer declined)
                              │
                              └──▶ Expired (validity date passed)
```

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | You're still building it. Not visible to the customer. |
| **Sent** | You marked it as sent. The customer received the link or PDF. |
| **Viewed** | The customer opened it (manually marked or via email tracking). |
| **Accepted** | The customer agreed to the price. Ready to convert. |
| **Rejected** | The customer declined. Keep it for the audit trail; don't reuse. |
| **Expired** | The validity date passed before the customer responded. |
| **Converted** | An order was created from this quote. The quote is locked. |

The status moves **forward only**. Once a quote is rejected or expired, you can't accept it — you have to clone it (a new draft with the same lines) and start over.

---

## What a quotation contains

Every quote carries these blocks:

- **Customer** — name, email, phone (snapshot, so later customer edits don't change the quote)
- **Lines** — each one is a product, quantity, and unit price
- **Totals** — subtotal, tax, shipping, discount, grand total
- **Validity** — a date after which the quote auto-expires
- **Notes** — free-form text the customer sees on the quote
- **Internal notes** — staff-only commentary that doesn't appear on the quote
- **Approvals** (optional) — for big-ticket quotes, route through internal sign-off before sending

---

## Quotation vs. Order — the difference

| Quotation | Order |
|---|---|
| Doesn't reserve stock | Reserves stock at placement |
| Doesn't trigger payment | Has a payment state (pending → paid) |
| Has a validity window | Has a fulfillment lifecycle (ship, deliver) |
| Customer can reject | Customer has already committed |
| Becomes an order on accept + convert | Is the source of truth for fulfillment |

A quote that's been **converted** has a back-reference to the order it produced. You can click through from the quote to the order it became.
