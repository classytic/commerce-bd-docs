# Expiry, Renewal & Cleanup

Quotes don't live forever. Here's how to handle the ones that go stale.

---

## How expiry works

Every quote can have a **validity date** (set when you create or edit the draft).

- Before the validity date: the quote is open. The customer can still accept.
- On or after the validity date: a background job marks the quote as **Expired** the next time it runs.

**Expired is terminal.** An expired quote can't be accepted. The customer would need a fresh quote.

If you don't set a validity date, the quote never auto-expires. It stays open until you manually expire or reject it.

---

## Manual expire

You can expire a quote at any time, before or after the validity date:

1. Open the quote
2. Click **Expire** (action menu)
3. Confirm

The status changes to **Expired** and `expiredAt` is recorded. Use this when:

- You decide not to honor the quote anymore (cost changes, supply issues)
- The customer has gone silent for too long
- You want to clean up a stale quote book

---

## Bulk expire (admin)

For cleanup, an admin can expire all quotes whose validity date has passed but are still showing as Sent or Viewed:

1. Go to **Quotations**
2. Filter to status **Sent** or **Viewed**, sort by **Valid Until** ascending
3. Pick the ones older than the cutoff and expire each, OR
4. (Future) Use a **Bulk Expire** action — currently expire one at a time

A nightly cron also auto-expires due quotes, so manual cleanup is mostly for prompt responses (e.g., "this should never have stayed open this long").

---

## When a customer wants a quote that has expired

Don't try to un-expire — that's not allowed. Instead:

### Clone the expired quote

1. Open the expired quote
2. Click **Clone** (creates a new draft with the same lines)
3. Refresh prices if the catalog has changed since
4. Set a new validity date
5. Send the new quote

The new quote gets a fresh number (e.g., `QUO-2026-0099` instead of `QUO-2026-0042`). The link from new → old is preserved in metadata for the audit trail.

### Negotiate fresh terms

Use the clone as the starting point. Common reasons to clone:

- Customer wants the same items but a longer lead time
- Customer wants a small modification (different variant, +1 item)
- The original quote covered the right scope but is just too old

---

## What about rejected quotes?

Rejected quotes work the same way as expired ones:

- They're terminal — can't be accepted
- You can clone them into a fresh draft if the customer comes back

The difference is intent — **rejected** means "the customer said no", **expired** means "we never heard back". Both are useful signals when reviewing your sales pipeline.

---

## Reporting on stale quotes

The quotations list shows status badges and validity dates. Useful filters:

| What you want | Filter |
|---|---|
| Quotes about to expire this week | Status: Sent or Viewed, Valid Until: this week |
| Quotes the customer ghosted | Status: Sent or Viewed, older than 14 days |
| Wins | Status: Converted |
| Lost | Status: Rejected |
| Stale drafts | Status: Draft, older than 7 days |

Sort by **last updated** to see what's been sitting and needs attention.
