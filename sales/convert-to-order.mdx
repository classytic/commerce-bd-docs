# Convert a Quote to an Order

When a customer accepts a quote, the next step is to turn it into a real order — a fulfillable record that reserves stock, takes payment, and ships goods.

---

## When to convert

Convert as soon as the quote is **Accepted**. Don't wait — converting:

- Locks the price (no more catalog drift)
- Reserves stock against future demand
- Starts the order's payment and fulfillment timer

If you wait days between accept and convert, two things can go wrong:

1. **Stock runs out** — someone else buys the same items in the meantime
2. **Catalog price changes** — the quote price is preserved on conversion, so this is safe, but only if you actually convert

---

## How to convert

1. Open an accepted quote
2. Click **Convert to order**
3. Confirm

You'll be redirected to the new order's detail page. The order number looks like `ORD-2026-0123`.

[Go to Quotations](/dashboard/quotations)

---

## What conversion produces

Conversion creates **one new order** with:

- **All the quote's lines** copied 1:1 — same products, same quantities, same agreed prices
- **The customer** (linked via the same customer record or snapshot)
- **The currency** from the quote
- **A back-reference** to the source quote — so you can trace the order back to its quote at any time

The original quote moves to **Converted** status and is **locked**:

- You can no longer edit it
- You can no longer change its lines
- It keeps a `convertedAt` timestamp and a link to the order

The quote becomes a permanent record of what was agreed.

---

## What conversion does NOT do

Conversion is just the order **creation** step. It does not:

- Take payment (the order starts in `payment.status: pending`)
- Reserve stock automatically (depends on your order config — most setups reserve at this step, but some defer to checkout)
- Ship anything (fulfillment is a separate step on the order)
- Send an invoice

These are the order's job, not the quote's. See the order's own actions for what's next.

---

## Idempotency: re-convert is safe-blocked

If you click **Convert** twice (double-click, network blip, page reload), only the first one creates an order. The second click returns an error like:

> Quotation QUO-2026-0042 has already been converted to ORD-2026-0123.

This is a safety mechanism. The quote can only produce one order, ever. If you actually need to fulfill the same quote a second time (rare — typically happens for repeat purchases by the same B2B customer):

- Clone the quote into a new draft
- Re-accept and re-convert

This keeps every order traceable to a single, distinct quote.

---

## Channel override on conversion

By default, the order inherits the quote's channel (`b2b`, `web`, etc.). You can override at convert time — useful when:

- A B2B quote actually gets fulfilled through your retail flow
- A walk-in negotiation results in an online-style fulfillment

The override only affects the new order. The quote keeps its original channel for reporting.

---

## What to do after converting

Once the order exists, the work moves to the **order detail page**:

1. **Payment** — record an advance, take a deposit, or send a payment link
2. **Fulfillment** — pick, pack, and ship (or mark as ready for pickup)
3. **Invoice** — generate a tax invoice (Mushak 6.3 if your business is VAT-registered)

The quote-to-order flow is now complete from the sales side. Everything downstream is normal order handling.
