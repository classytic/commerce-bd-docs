# Tiers

Tiers are the **membership levels** in your loyalty program. Each tier has its own earning multiplier, discount %, and other perks. Members move up automatically when they hit qualification thresholds.

[Open Tiers](/dashboard/loyalty/tiers)

---

## Anatomy of a Tier

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Name** | Display label (e.g. "Silver", "Gold", "Platinum") |
| **Rank** | Numeric ordering — 1 is the lowest tier |
| **Min lifetime points** | Optional — qualify by points earned (per evaluation period) |
| **Min lifetime spend** | Optional — qualify by BDT spent (per evaluation period) |
| **Earning multiplier** | Applies on top of every earning rule (e.g. 1.5×) |
| **Discount %** | Auto-discount on every order (e.g. 5%) |
| **Free shipping** | Toggle — when on, this tier always ships free |
| **Custom benefits** | Free-form key/value (e.g. `priority_support: true`) |
| **Downgrade grace** | Days at lower spend before the member drops down (default: 30) |

You can set EITHER min points OR min spend OR both — at least one is required.

---

## Create a Tier

Go to **Loyalty > Tiers** and click **New Tier**. Fill in the fields above and save. The tier appears in the list ordered by rank.

A typical setup:

| Tier | Rank | Min lifetime points (90d) | Earning × | Discount % | Free shipping |
|------|------|---------------------------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Bronze | 1 | 0 | 1.0× | 0% | No |
| Silver | 2 | 1,000 | 1.2× | 2% | No |
| Gold | 3 | 5,000 | 1.5× | 5% | Yes |
| Platinum | 4 | 20,000 | 2.0× | 10% | Yes |

## How Qualification Works

Tier evaluation runs **periodically** (typical: every 90 days). The system looks at the member's earned points (or spend) over the **last evaluation period** and finds the highest tier they qualify for.

Three things to know:

1. **Lifetime != all-time.** "Lifetime points" means points earned in the **evaluation window**, not since enrollment. A member who earned 5000 points last year but only 200 this quarter drops to whatever tier 200 qualifies for.

2. **Downgrade is gentle.** When a member's qualification drops below the current tier's threshold, they enter a **grace period** (default 30 days). If they earn enough during the grace period to re-qualify, they stay. Otherwise they drop one tier at the end of grace.

3. **Override beats auto.** An admin tier override (set on the member's profile) bypasses auto-evaluation entirely. Use for VIPs, partner accounts, or correcting bugs.

---

## Re-Evaluate All Members

If you change qualification thresholds (e.g. raise Silver from 500 → 1000 lifetime points), existing members aren't downgraded immediately. Click **Evaluate All** on the Tiers page to recompute everyone's tier with the new rules.

This:
- Walks every member
- Computes their qualifying period stats
- Updates tier assignments
- Logs each change in the audit ledger

A few minutes for thousands of members; longer for hundreds of thousands. Run during off-peak hours.

---

## Benefits in Action

When a member checks out, the system:

1. Looks up their current tier
2. Applies the **earning multiplier** to whatever earning rules fire
3. Applies the **discount %** to the order subtotal
4. Skips shipping cost if **free shipping** is on
5. Includes any **custom benefits** flags in the cart payload (your front-end can read these to show "Priority pickup available" etc.)

You don't need to set up matching cart promotions — tier benefits apply automatically based on the customer's loyalty record.

## Common Mistakes

| Symptom | Likely cause |
|---------|--------------|
| Members keep dropping every quarter | Threshold too high relative to typical purchase rate |
| Everyone is in the top tier | Threshold too low; raise it and run **Evaluate All** |
| Promotion launched, nobody got upgraded | Forgot to **Evaluate All** after changing thresholds |
| VIP customer downgraded by mistake | Use a **Tier Override** with a reason instead of relying on auto |
