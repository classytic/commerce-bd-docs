# Cycle Counting & Audit

A **stock audit** physically counts what's actually in your warehouse and reconciles it against system stock. The system supports three audit types and posts variance adjustments to the GL automatically.

[Open Audit](/dashboard/warehouse/audit)

---

## Three Count Types

| Type | What it covers | When to use |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| **Full** | Every SKU at every location | Year-end, pre-IPO audit, post-major-event |
| **Cycle** | A subset (specific locations / categories / SKUs) | Monthly rotation — count 1/12 of inventory each month |
| **Spot** | One or a few SKUs | Investigate a specific variance or shrinkage report |

Cycle counts are the **right default** for most businesses. Full counts are disruptive (often require closing the shop); spot counts are reactive.

---

## Anatomy of an Audit Session

| Field | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Count type** | full / cycle / spot |
| **Scope** | nodeId (warehouse), locationId, skuRefs (which SKUs to include) |
| **Freeze policy** | `hard` (block all moves), `soft` (warn but allow), `none` (no freeze — risky) |
| **Status** | `planned` → `counting` → `reconciling` → `closed` |
| **Lines** | One per (SKU, location) — counted qty + variance reason |
| **Created by / closed by** | Audit trail |

---

## Step 1 — Plan the Audit

1. **Warehouse > Audit** → **New Audit**
2. Pick:
   - **Count type** (full / cycle / spot)
   - **Scope** — which warehouse + locations + SKUs (leave blank = all)
   - **Freeze policy** — `hard` for accuracy, `soft` for low disruption
3. Save as `planned`

This pre-creates the line items (one row per SKU+location in scope) but doesn't freeze anything yet.

---

## Step 2 — Start Counting

Click **Start** on the audit. The status moves to `counting`. Depending on freeze policy:

- **hard** — all stock moves on these SKU+locations are blocked. Sales, transfers, adjustments fail with "audit in progress" until the audit closes.
- **soft** — moves are allowed but logged with a warning. Useful when you can't shut down operations.
- **none** — no freeze. Variance will include any moves that happened during the count. Avoid unless you really know what you're doing.

Hand the count sheet (or a scanner-loaded device) to the warehouse staff. They walk the locations, count physical units, record the number.

---

## Step 3 — Submit Lines

For each SKU+location, record:

```
POST /audits/:id/lines {
  skuRef: 'TSHIRT-RED-M',
  locationId: 'loc_main_a3',
  countedQuantity: 47,
  varianceReason: 'damaged in shelf',  // optional
  lotId: 'lot_20260115',  // if lot-tracked
}
```

Or use the dashboard line entry — same fields.

The system computes variance per line:

```
variance = countedQuantity − systemQuantity
```

- Positive variance → more on shelf than system thought (over)
- Negative → less on shelf (short — possible shrinkage)
- Zero → perfect

---

## Step 4 — Reconcile

Once all lines are counted, click **Reconcile**. The status moves to `reconciling`. The system shows:

- Total lines counted
- Lines with variance ≠ 0
- Sum of variance × cost (the financial impact)
- Auto-approve threshold (configurable per audit, e.g. 2 units)

Decide what to do:

- **Auto-approve small variances** — variances ≤ threshold post adjustment moves automatically
- **Manual review for large variances** — investigate before posting

For each line in manual review, you can:

- **Approve** — post the adjustment as-is
- **Edit** — correct the counted quantity (e.g. miscount discovered)
- **Reject** — set count back to system value (e.g. count was wrong, system was right)

Click **Confirm Reconciliation** when done. The audit moves to `closed`.

---

## What Posts to GL

For each approved variance line, the system creates an **adjustment move**:

```
DR  Inventory account     OR    DR  Inventory Variance
CR  Inventory Variance          CR  Inventory account
```

Direction depends on sign:
- Positive variance (over) → credits Variance, debits Inventory (Inventory goes UP)
- Negative variance (short) → debits Variance, credits Inventory (Inventory goes DOWN)

The variance account reflects on the P&L as a one-time gain/loss. Significant losses warrant investigation (theft? recording errors? damaged goods?).

## Freeze Policy — Choosing Right

| Policy | Pro | Con | Best for |
|--------|-----|-----|----------|
| **hard** | Most accurate count (no movements during count) | Disrupts operations — no sales, no transfers | Full audits, year-end |
| **soft** | Operations continue | Variance includes mid-count movements | Cycle counts during business hours |
| **none** | No friction at all | Variance is unreliable | Spot checks where you only care about gross numbers |

For high-value SKUs, always `hard` — even brief intermissions to count 100 expensive items are worth the certainty.

---

## Common Patterns

**Monthly rotation cycle counting** — schedule 12 cycle audits, one per month, each covering 1/12 of SKUs (rotated by category or location). Over a year, every SKU gets counted at least once.

**ABC analysis** — count high-value SKUs (A) every month, mid-value (B) quarterly, low-value (C) annually. Spend audit budget where it matters.

**Pre/post-event audit** — count before a sale event, count after. Net difference + sales = expected; anything else is shrinkage.

**Spot-check on shrinkage report** — your reports flag a specific SKU as having dropped faster than sales explain. Run a spot audit on that SKU at all locations to confirm.

---

## Common Pitfalls

| Symptom | Cause + fix |
|---------|-------------|
| Variance huge across the board | Probably a calibration issue — units of measure (e.g. counting in cases vs each), wrong location scope. Re-count a sample to verify. |
| Variance changes after reconciliation | Something else moved stock after you submitted lines. Use `hard` freeze next time. |
| Cycle count freeze blocks legitimate sales | Use `soft` freeze, or split the audit into smaller scopes that finish faster |
| Shrinkage trend keeps appearing in same location | Investigate the location — possible theft, damage, wrong-bin storage. Camera + access log review. |
