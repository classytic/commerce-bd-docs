# Credit & A/R Ledger

For B2B accounts that buy on credit (net-30, net-60, etc.) you need to:

1. **Enable credit** with a limit and payment days
2. Optionally **post an opening balance** if migrating from another system
3. **Issue invoices** that post to the A/R ledger automatically
4. **Record payments** as they come in
5. **Watch the aged buckets** to chase overdue customers

[Open Customers](/dashboard/customers)

---

## Step 1 — Enable Credit

On the customer profile, scroll to the **Credit Terms** card:

- Toggle **Credit enabled** on
- Set **Credit limit** in BDT (leave blank for unlimited — not recommended)
- Set **Credit days** (e.g. `30` for net-30)
- Click **Save**

The card warns: *"Limit: X BDT. Invoices over cap will be rejected at post time."* This is a real enforcement — see below.

> Credit limit enforcement requires `ENABLE_CREDIT_LIMIT=true` on the backend. Confirm with your platform admin if invoices aren't being blocked as expected.

---

## How the Limit Enforces

When a credit-sale invoice is about to post, the system checks:

```
outstanding A/R balance + new invoice amount  ≤  credit limit
```

If not, the invoice **rejects** with an error like:
> Invoice would push customer over credit limit (BDT 60,000 of 50,000)

The invoice stays in `draft` status — fix it by:
- Reducing the invoice amount
- Receiving a payment first to free up credit
- Raising the customer's limit (if approved by finance)

Cash sales aren't affected — only credit sales hit the limit.

## Step 2 — Post an Opening Balance (Migration Only)

If this customer already owes you money from before you went live:

1. Customer profile → **A/R Ledger** tab
2. Click **Opening Balance** at the top
3. Enter the amount in BDT + a reference (e.g. "Pre-go-live balance from QuickBooks")
4. Save

The system posts a journal entry: **DR A/R 1210**, **CR Retained Earnings 3310**. It's **idempotent** — clicking the button twice does nothing the second time. Safe to retry.

You only do this **once per customer**. Subsequent invoices accumulate normally.

## The A/R Ledger Tab

This tab is your accounts-receivable cockpit for the customer. Top to bottom:

### Balance Summary

Four cards: **Opening**, **Closing**, **Open items total**, **Matched total**. The closing minus matched equals what's still owed and unpaid.

### Open Invoices

Every unpaid invoice with: invoice number, date, due date, amount, days overdue. Two actions per row:

- **Receive Payment** — record a payment (see next section)
- **Debit Note** — issue an allowance / partial credit (e.g. "we shipped wrong color, BDT 500 off")

### Statement Table

Full ledger entries — every JE that touched A/R for this customer, in order, with running balance. Use the date range picker at the top to narrow the period.

### Aged Buckets

Color-coded breakdown:
- **0–30 days** (current — green)
- **30–60 days** (chase soon — yellow)
- **60–90 days** (chase now — orange)
- **90+ days** (collections concern — red)

Use this for credit-control conversations: "You have BDT 12,000 in 90+ days bucket — when can we expect payment?"

## Step 3 — Record a Payment

When the customer pays:

1. Open the customer's A/R Ledger tab
2. On the open invoice they're paying, click **Receive Payment**
3. Enter:
   - **Amount received** in BDT
   - **Payment method** — cash, bank transfer, bKash, etc.
   - **Reference** — the bank reference / wallet TXN ID
   - **Date received** (defaults to today)
4. Save

The system:
- Posts a JE: **DR Cash/Bank**, **CR A/R 1210**
- Marks the invoice as **paid** (full) or **partially paid** (if amount < invoice total)
- The closing balance drops by the payment amount

For payments that cover **multiple invoices** at once, you can either:
- Record them one-at-a-time (simplest)
- Use **batch settlement** (if your account team has set it up) — match a single bank deposit to N invoices

## Issue a Debit Note (Customer Owes You More)

For situations where the customer owes additional money (e.g. you under-billed):

1. Open the customer's A/R Ledger tab
2. Click **Debit Note** on the relevant invoice
3. Enter:
   - **Amount** in BDT
   - **Reason** (required — appears in the audit trail)
   - **Reference** (optional, e.g. an internal ticket number)
4. Save

A debit note **adds to** the customer's A/R balance. The opposite is a **credit note** — see [Credit & Debit Notes](/commerce-bd/docs/accounting/credit-debit-notes) for the full pattern.

## Common Pitfalls

| Symptom | Cause + fix |
|---------|-------------|
| Invoice fails to post: "would exceed credit limit" | Customer is at the cap. Receive a payment first or raise the limit. |
| Opening balance dialog won't save | A previous opening balance already exists. Idempotent — safe to ignore. |
| Aged buckets look wrong | Check the date range filter at the top; "today" is the as-of date for aging. |
| Customer says they paid, ledger shows owing | Search by reference number on the customer's statement. If not there, the payment was logged to the wrong customer or never posted. |
