# A/P Ledger

The supplier's A/P Ledger tab is your **accounts payable cockpit** for that vendor. It holds every bill they've sent, every payment you've made, and every credit note you've issued — with running balance and aged buckets.

[Open Suppliers](/dashboard/suppliers)

---

## The Layout

Open the supplier → click the **A/P Ledger** tab. Top to bottom:

### Date range filter
Default: Jan 1 to today. Narrow to a quarter, month, or specific dates for period reporting.

### Balance summary cards
Four cards: **Opening**, **Closing**, **Open items total**, **Matched total**. Closing minus matched = what's still owed and unpaid.

### Open Bills table
Every unpaid bill with: bill number, date, due date, amount, days overdue. Two actions per row:

- **Pay Bill** — record a payment from cash or bank
- **Credit Note** — allowance / return for a previously-billed amount

### Statement table
Full ledger entries — every JE that touched A/P for this supplier, in order, with running balance.

### Aged Buckets
Color-coded breakdown:
- **0–30 days** — current (green)
- **31–60 days** — chase soon (yellow)
- **60–90 days** — pay this week (orange)
- **90+ days** — late payment penalty risk (red)

Use this for cashflow planning: "How much do we need to pay in the next 30 days?" → sum of 0–30 + 31–60 buckets.

## Pay a Bill

When you're ready to settle a vendor bill:

1. Open the supplier's A/P Ledger tab
2. Find the bill in the Open Bills table
3. Click **Pay Bill**
4. Enter:
   - **Amount** — full bill amount or partial (multiple payments OK)
   - **Source account** — which cash/bank account funds this payment
   - **Payment date** — defaults to today
   - **Reference** — your bank's transaction ID, cheque number, etc.
   - **Notes** (optional)
5. Save

The system posts a JE: **DR A/P 2111**, **CR Cash/Bank**. The bill marks as **paid** (full) or **partial** (if amount < bill total). The closing balance drops.

### Multi-bill batch payment

If you're sending one bank transfer covering 5 bills:

- Option A: Record 5 separate payments, each with the same reference → simple, all bills marked paid
- Option B: Use **batch settlement** (if your finance team set it up) — single payment line that allocates to multiple bills

Most teams default to Option A — slower but unambiguous in the audit trail.

## Issue a Credit Note (Vendor Owes You)

When the vendor owes you back money — return, allowance, billing error:

1. Open the supplier's A/P Ledger tab
2. Find the bill the credit relates to → click **Credit Note**
3. Enter:
   - **Amount** in BDT
   - **Reason** (required — appears in audit)
   - **Reference** (e.g. internal RMA ticket, return tracking number)
4. Save

The system posts: **DR A/P 2111**, **CR Inventory/Expense Adjustment**. The supplier's payable balance drops by the credit note amount. If you've already paid the bill, this becomes a **refund receivable** — track it via the supplier's notes until they actually refund you.

For full credit-debit-note pattern, see [Credit & Debit Notes](/commerce-bd/docs/accounting/credit-debit-notes).

## Reading the Aged Buckets

A typical end-of-month view:

```
0–30 days:  BDT 850,000   ← current bills, pay on schedule
31–60 days: BDT 220,000   ← past terms, chase to pay this week
60–90 days: BDT  45,000   ← significantly late, vendor relationship at risk
90+ days:   BDT  12,000   ← collections risk for the vendor; pay or contest
```

Run this view weekly. Goal: **shrink the right tail**. Anything older than 30 days = either a real dispute (resolve it) or process failure (find and fix it).

## Reconciliation Tips

When the vendor's statement disagrees with yours:

1. Filter the statement table by date range matching the vendor's
2. Compare: count of bills, sum of bills, count of payments, sum of payments
3. The difference = the dispute. Common causes:
   - Bill they sent but you never received → ask for re-send
   - Payment you made but they didn't apply → send your bank reference
   - Credit note you issued but they didn't apply → forward your debit note
4. Once reconciled, post any missing entries from your side, ask them to post missing ones from theirs

## Common Pitfalls

| Symptom | Cause + fix |
|---------|-------------|
| "Pay Bill" button missing | The bill is already fully paid OR you lack `finance-manager` role |
| Statement shows debit where I expect credit | Check the JE direction — debit notes increase A/P (you owe more), credits decrease |
| Closing balance ≠ open items total | Open items only counts unpaid bills. Closing = opening + all postings (including matched payments). They should differ by the matched total. |
| Bill from previous fiscal year still appearing | Check date range filter — it defaults to current FY. Stretch the start date back further. |
