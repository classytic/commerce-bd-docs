# Park & Resume

Customer at the counter asks to run back to their car. Another customer is waiting. You don't want to lose the first cart's items, discount, loyalty lookup. **Park** it.

---

## How it works

Each cashier can park **up to 5 orders** simultaneously. Parked orders live in the browser's localStorage for the current shift, scoped to the active branch. Each one preserves:

- Cart items (products, variants, quantities)
- Customer selection (name, phone, membership card)
- Manual discount / voucher code state

When you resume, the cart loads exactly as you left it. Pricing re-runs against the current catalog at resume time — if a product was re-priced since park, the resumed cart shows the new price.

---

## Park an order

From the products screen with a cart in progress:

1. Click **Park** in the cart panel (or type the shortcut if wired on your install)
2. Optional: give it a **label** to find it later (e.g. "red shirt lady")
3. Confirm

The cart clears, you're back on products ready for the next customer. The parked order appears in the **Parked** menu in the top bar with a counter (e.g. "Parked (2)").

---

## Resume a parked order

1. Top bar → **Parked** menu
2. Click the order you want — cart refills
3. Continue — add items, checkout, or further modify

Resuming **moves** the parked order into the active cart — it's removed from the parked list. If you park the resumed cart again, it becomes a new entry (so labels may differ).

---

## Delete a parked order

From the **Parked** menu, click the ✕ next to an order. Gone. No confirmation for a single delete — it's a quick action.

**Clear all** at the bottom of the menu wipes all parked orders at once (this one does confirm).

---

## Five-slot limit

The 6th park attempt fails with **"Maximum 5 parked orders. Resume or delete one first."**

Why the cap: parked orders in localStorage add up — each can carry dozens of cart lines with full product snapshots. 5 is the limit that keeps performance tight and the Parked menu scannable.

If you're hitting the limit regularly, that's a workflow signal. Either resume faster, or your store has a queueing problem that parking can't solve.

---

## Expiry — 8 hours

Parked orders auto-expire **8 hours after park** (a typical shift length). The next time you open the Parked menu after expiry, stale ones are purged silently.

Why: parked cart's product prices, promos, and stock may have shifted overnight. Auto-expiry prevents yesterday's ghost sale from showing up today. If you genuinely need to save a cart longer, take a photo of the screen — don't rely on park.

---

## What parking does NOT do

| Not this | Do this instead |
|---|---|
| Save an order across shifts | Complete the sale, or have the customer return tomorrow |
| Reserve stock | Park doesn't decrement inventory. If a parked cart holds the last item, another cashier can still sell it. |
| Share across terminals | Parked orders are per-browser. Log in on another terminal and you won't see them. |
| Park with partial payment | Parking is for pre-payment carts. After any tender, the order is real — refund it if the customer walks out. |

## Common use cases

- **Customer stepped away** — ring up others while they return
- **Need to fetch an item from the back** — park, serve next customer, resume when back
- **Product lookup in progress** — you're finding the right SKU for a tricky request; park the current cart while you search
- **Comparing options** — customer is deciding between two products; park a cart with option A, build a cart with option B, show them side-by-side receipts

## Troubleshooting

**"My parked orders disappeared"**
- You cleared browser storage, used incognito, or logged into a different user
- Parked orders are per-browser, per-user, per-branch
- No recovery path — parking is volatile by design

**"A parked order shows the wrong price"**
- Pricing runs at resume time, not at park time
- If the product was re-priced between park and resume, the new price shows
- The item list itself (which products, what qty, which variant) is preserved

**"Can I park an order with a redeemed voucher?"**
- Yes — the voucher code is preserved in the parked cart
- On resume, the voucher re-validates (still active? still within expiry?) — if it fails, a warning shows and the discount drops

---

## Permissions

Anyone with POS access can park + resume their own orders. There's no role gate — parking is a cashier convenience, not a financial action.
