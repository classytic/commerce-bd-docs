# Opening a Shift

Every cashier day starts here. Takes about 30 seconds.

[Open the POS terminal](/pos)

---

## Before You Click "Open"

Three checks:

1. **Right branch** — confirm the branch switcher in the top bar shows the store you're working in. The shift is tied to that branch — opening at the wrong one means your sales count toward the wrong store's report.
2. **Right cashier login** — confirm you're logged in as yourself (your initials in the top-right). Shifts are tied to the user ID; the Z-report at end-of-day shows your name.
3. **Drawer reset** — the physical cash drawer should be at zero (no leftover from yesterday). If there's residual cash, count it and either reconcile it now or hand it to the manager.

---

## Step 1 — Count the Float

The **float** is the opening cash that goes into the drawer so you can make change from the very first sale. Most stores standardize this (e.g. always BDT 1,000 in mixed denominations).

A typical float:

| Denomination | Count | Total |
|--------------|-------|-------|
| 100 BDT × 5 | 5 | 500 |
| 50 BDT × 6 | 6 | 300 |
| 20 BDT × 5 | 5 | 100 |
| 10 BDT × 5 | 5 | 50 |
| 5 BDT × 5 | 5 | 25 |
| 2 BDT × 12 | 12 | 24 |
| 1 BDT × 1 | 1 | 1 |
| **Total** | | **1,000** |

Count it twice. Match the manager's expected float for the day. Discrepancies before opening are easier to catch than at close.

---

## Step 2 — Open the Register

1. Click **POS** in the sidebar (or hit `/pos` directly)
2. The screen lands on **Shift Open** (because no shift is currently active)
3. Type the float amount you counted (e.g. `1000`)
4. Click **Open Register**

The system creates a shift record:

- **Status** = `open`
- **Opening cash** = your float amount
- **Opened at** = current timestamp
- **Cashier** = your user
- **Branch** = active branch

You're immediately taken to the **Products** screen — ready to sell.

---

## What If a Shift Is Already Open?

If you click **POS** and the screen shows the Products screen directly (not Shift Open), there's already an open shift for this branch. Three possibilities:

**You opened it yourself yesterday and didn't close.** Close it now (F10), reconcile yesterday's drawer if possible, then open today's shift.

**Another cashier opened it earlier.** They're using the same branch. Either:
- Take over their shift (their sales become part of yours — usually wrong)
- Have them close, then you open fresh

**A previous shift crashed without closing.** You'll see the open shift's start time and cashier in the X-report. Have a manager close it (entering whatever cash IS in the drawer as the closing count) so you can start fresh.

There's no "force-open" — only one shift at a time per branch.

---

## Manager Float Override

If the day's expected float is unusual (extra cash for a sale event, less cash for a weekday morning), a manager can:

1. Enter their own login PIN at the float prompt
2. Override the standard float to whatever's appropriate
3. Add a reason in the optional notes field

The override is logged in the shift's audit trail. The closing variance still uses whatever opening cash was actually entered — no shenanigans.

---

## What's Recorded

When you open a shift, the system stores:

- **Cashier** — your user ID
- **Branch** — the active org
- **Opening cash** — the float you counted
- **Opened at** — timestamp
- **Cash movements** — empty array (any in/out movements during the shift land here)
- **Sales count + total** — start at 0, increment as you ring up sales
- **Refund count + total** — start at 0
- **Status** — `open`

Everything you do during the shift writes to this record.
