# Closing Shift & Reports

Closing the shift is a 2-minute process that produces the **Z-report** — your end-of-day record of sales, cash, and variance.

[Open the POS terminal](/pos)

---

## When to Close

- End of the cashier's work session
- End of the business day (typical for single-shift stores)
- Shift handover to the next cashier (each cashier closes theirs, the next opens fresh)

Don't leave shifts open overnight if you can help it — overnight shifts blur the daily-close line for accounting and complicate variance investigation.

---

## Step 1 — Trigger Close

Click **Close Register** in the top bar. There's no keyboard shortcut — close is deliberate, always a click.

The screen switches to **Shift Close** showing:

- **Opened at** — when the shift started
- **Cashier** — your name
- **Sales count** — how many transactions
- **Sales total** — gross revenue
- **Refund count + total** — if any
- **Expected cash** — `opening + cash sales − cash refunds + cash movements`

The "expected cash" is what should be in your drawer right now, based on every transaction logged during the shift.

> If your branch has **blind close** policy enabled, you won't see expected cash here — just the count-the-drawer input. See [Blind Close](#blind-close) below and the full [Shift Policies](/pos/shift-policies) reference.

---

## Step 2 — Count the Drawer

Physically count every note and coin in the drawer. The screen shows a numpad — type the actual amount.

Take your time. Re-count any denomination you're not sure about. Once you submit, you can't go back.

> **Tip:** Before counting, set aside the float (the opening cash you started with) and count it separately. Then the rest is "cash from sales" — easier to verify against the expected number on screen.

---

## Step 3 — Variance

The system computes:

```
variance = actual cash counted − expected cash
```

| Variance | Meaning | Action |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **0** | Drawer is exact | Save and close — perfect |
| **Positive** (over) | More cash than expected | Possible: missed recording a refund, customer overpaid and you didn't give change. Investigate, document. |
| **Negative** (short) | Less cash than expected | Possible: change given incorrectly, theft, missed entry. **Always investigate.** |

For non-zero variance, the screen prompts for a **note** explaining the difference. Examples:

- "Customer paid 1000, total was 850, gave 100 change — short 50, customer left before correction"
- "Found extra 100 BDT in drawer, no record of source — flagged for manager review"

---

## Step 4 — Confirm Close

Click **Close Register**. The system:

1. Sets `shift.status = closed`
2. Stamps `closedAt = now`
3. Records `closingCash`, `expectedCash`, `cashDifference`
4. Saves your variance note
5. Generates the **Z-report** (downloadable from the close screen)
6. Returns to the Shift Open screen, ready for the next session

The shift record is now **immutable** — corrections must be done via cash journal entries, not by reopening the shift.

---

## The Z-Report

The Z-report is the official end-of-shift summary. It includes:

- **Cashier name + branch + shift dates/times**
- **Sales** — count, gross revenue, VAT, net
- **Refunds** — count, total
- **Discounts** — auto-promo total, manual discount total, loyalty redemption total
- **Payments** — breakdown by method (cash, bKash, Nagad, card, etc.)
- **Cash reconciliation** — opening, cash sales, cash refunds, expected, counted, variance
- **Notes** — your variance explanation (if any)

Print one copy, file it. Some businesses also email a copy to head office finance.

---

## What Happens to the Data

After close:

| Where | What posts |
|-------|------------|
| **Orders module** | Each sale appears as a regular order — same as online orders. Filter by `channel: pos` to see POS only. |
| **Inventory** | Stock decrements happen at sale time (already done before close). |
| **Loyalty** | Points earned and redeemed (already done at sale time). |
| **GL / Accounting** | Revenue, VAT, COGS, cash entries posted asynchronously per transaction. |
| **Cash journal** | Opening cash, total cash sales, total cash refunds, closing cash variance — all reconcile to the cash GL account. |
| **Reports** | Sales reports, variance trends, cashier performance — all updated nightly. |

You don't trigger any of this manually — the order engine wires it all up.

## Investigating Variance

Variance > 0.5% of sales is worth a look. Variance > 2% is a problem.

Common causes:

| Cause | How to detect | Fix |
|-------|---------------|-----|
| **Wrong change given** | Look for cash sales near round numbers (e.g. paid 1000 for 850 → owed 150 in change, gave 100) | Re-train cashier on change-counting |
| **Off-by-one quantity** | Compare receipt count to actual items left | Use the scanner more, less manual entry |
| **Discount applied without recording** | Look at high-discount sales | Require manager auth for discounts > 5% |
| **Refund not entered** | Customer remembered taking item back, you forgot to refund | Always F9-Refund at the moment of return |
| **Cash drawer left open** | Theft window during busy periods | Auto-close drawer after every sale |

For repeated variances from one cashier, manager-level review of the X-reports during their shift can pinpoint the moment things drifted.

## Manager Override (When Variance Won't Reconcile)

When variance falls outside the branch's configured thresholds (default: ৳100 absolute OR 0.5% of expected — either keeps you safe, **both** failing triggers override), the close is blocked at the API layer with `403 MANAGER_OVERRIDE_REQUIRED`.

**What the cashier sees**:
- Inline "Manager required" prompt
- Reason textarea (required)
- Credentials entry for a user with manager role

**After manager auth**:
1. The variance saves to the shift record
2. A note is added: "Manager override: <reason>" + manager user id
3. The cash journal posts a **variance line** (Dr / Cr `Cash Over/Short` account) so the trial balance still ties out
4. Recurring overrides flag the cashier in cashier-performance reports

Don't abuse this — every override is investigated by the finance team, and the Z-report clearly shows the override flag.

Thresholds are configurable per branch — see [Shift Policies](/pos/shift-policies) for `varianceThresholdAbs`, `varianceThresholdPct`, and `managerOverrideRequired`.

## Blind Close

When your branch policy has `blindCloseRequired: true`, the close flow changes:

**For the cashier**:
1. The close screen shows **no expected cash** — just the count input
2. Count the drawer, enter the number, submit
3. Shift transitions to **`blind_closed`** (not `closed`)
4. Cashier is done — they can log out

**Later, for the manager**:
1. Open **Reports → Open Shifts → Blind-Closed**
2. Review opening, sales, movements
3. Enter the reconcile count (often the manager re-counts)
4. Confirm — shift transitions to **`closed`**
5. Variance is recorded at reconcile time, not at cashier close

**Why this exists**: prevents cashiers from "counting to the expected number" — a form of theft where an experienced cashier knows exactly how much they need to fake-count to match. With blind close, they genuinely have to count what's there.

Cost: slower close. Most branches don't need this — use it for high-volume or high-risk stores.

## Handover (pause and resume)

When branch policy has `allowHandover: true`, the cashier can **pause** a shift instead of closing:

1. **Pause Shift** button (next to Close Register)
2. Shift transitions to `paused` — drawer rejects new sales
3. Next cashier logs in and clicks **Resume Shift** — it reopens for them

The shift record tracks both cashiers. The Z-report at eventual close shows the handover moment + who worked which hours.

**Use for**:
- Lunch breaks (short pause, same cashier resumes)
- Morning/evening shift change at the same register
- Temporary absence where you don't want to close + reopen (loses the opening-float setup overhead)

**Don't use for**:
- Multi-day shifts — just close and re-open the next day
- Across-branch handover — shifts are branch-scoped

See [Shift Policies](/pos/shift-policies) for the `allowHandover` setting.

---

## Auto-close (abandoned shifts)

Sometimes a cashier walks out without closing. By default, shifts stay open indefinitely — bad for day-close accounting.

When branch policy has `autoCloseEnabled: true`, a cron job auto-closes open shifts past `autoCloseTime` (e.g. 23:59 Dhaka). The auto-close:

- Sets `closingCash = expectedCash` (zero variance by definition)
- Transitions the shift to **`orphaned_closed`** (distinct from normal `closed`)
- Flags the shift for next-morning manager review
- Notifies the branch manager

The manager's job next morning: inspect the orphaned shift, count the physical drawer, record actual variance via cash journal adjustments. The `orphaned_closed` tag prevents it from being confused with a genuine close in reports.
